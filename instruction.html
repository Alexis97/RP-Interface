Recurring Pattern

A Recurring Pattern (RP) is an ensemble of similar yet not necessarily identical "instances" in an image.

The term of "instances" refers to a visual area which distinctive features that could be undoubtedly grouped or segmented together. Each recurring pattern consists of two or more similar instances.

An example image contains ONE recurring pattern.

An example image contains Multiple recurring patterns.

The goal of the study is to find out how humans perceive recurring patterns in real word scenes.

Instructions:

On each image:

  • If you see a group of similar instances as a recurring pattern, please click the Polygon button (hotkey: "p") on the bottom panel to draw the contour of each instance respectively. All the instances belonging to the same recurring pattern should be labeled by the SAME color of polygon.
  • After that,
    • If you cannot observe any more recurring pattern, please click the Submit button on the right side of the bottom panel to submit your labeling and go to the next image.
    • If you observe some other recurring pattern, please click the Add button on the top of the right-most panel. After that, you should see a new Recurring Pattern with a different color appears on the right panel. Select this recurring pattern, and do the labeling as you did for the first recurring pattern. Make sure you label the new recurring pattern with a different color of polygon.
    • Do this repeatedly until no more recurring patterns. If you cannot observe any more recurring pattern, please click the Submit button on the right side of the bottom panel to submit your labeling and go to the next image.
  • You can skip the image if you cannot observe any recurring pattern based on your perception. Please select "Nothing to label" next to the Submit button on the right side of the bottom panel, to skip this image and go to the next one.
