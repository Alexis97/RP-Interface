A Basic Introduction to Recurring Pattern

What is Recurring Pattern?

To answer this basic question, let's firstly take a look at some examples:

These milk bottles, chip bags, paintings, animals and even human beings share quite a lot in common. Specifically, there are a series of **instances** which share similar appearance and geometric properties in each image. A **recurring pattern (RP)** is an ensemble of similar yet not necessarily identical **instances** in an image.

It is trivial to notice that any RP should contain **two or more instances**, otherwise a single instance can hardly to tell similar or not.

Now let's give a definition:

  • A Recurring Pattern (RP) is an ensemble of similar yet not necessarily identical "instances" in an image.
  • The term of "instances" refers to a visual area which distinctive features that could be undoubtedly grouped or segmented together. Each recurring pattern consists of two or more similar instances.
