A Quick Review of Recurring Pattern Concepts

What is a Recurring Pattern?

A Recurring Pattern (RP) is an ensemble of similar yet not necessarily identical "instances" in an image.

What is an Instance?

The term of "instances" refers to a visual area which distinctive features that could be undoubtedly grouped or segmented together. Each recurring pattern consists of two or more similar instances.

How many Recurring Patterns can an image contains?

An image can contains one dominant recurring pattern, or multiple recurring patterns. It all depends!

Q: What if I find more/less Recurring Patterns then the others? Am I wrong?

A: Don't worry! The discovery of recurring pattern is totally based on your own perception! It is totally okay if you treat some instances as recurring patterns but the others do not think so. :)

Q: Can I view any arbitrary patterns as a recurring pattern?

A: No. A recurring pattern should contain some kind of similarity that shares among all its instances. So you cannot just arbitrarily pick patterns as a recurring pattern.
